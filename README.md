# Substratum

**Substratum** is a sandbox game centered around procedural world generation, resource extraction, construction systems, and open-ended exploration. It emphasizes player-driven progression through iterative interaction with the environment rather than combat or survival mechanics.

## Overview

Substratum places players in a dynamically generated world composed of layered terrain, resource distributions, and distinct environmental regions. The primary gameplay loop revolves around gathering materials, constructing functional and aesthetic structures, and expanding into new areas as capabilities improve.

The experience is intentionally non-combative, focusing instead on creativity, optimization, and exploration.

## Core Systems

### Resource Extraction

* Procedurally distributed ores and materials across terrain layers
* Surface and subterranean resource nodes
* Tool-based harvesting with progression-based efficiency improvements

### Construction System

* Modular building mechanics for structural design
* Material-based variation in appearance and function
* Scalable base development supporting storage, organization, and expansion

### World Exploration

* Multiple biome types with unique terrain composition
* Underground cave networks and vertical progression
* Resource gating that encourages deeper or broader exploration

### Progression Loop

* Extract resources to unlock crafting options
* Craft tools to increase gathering efficiency and access new materials
* Expand infrastructure to support larger-scale operations
* Continue exploration to discover new resource tiers

## Design Philosophy

Substratum is designed as a systems-driven sandbox with minimal constraints on player behavior. The absence of combat shifts focus toward:

* Creative construction and spatial design
* Resource optimization and routing
* Exploration-driven discovery
* Iterative progression without failure penalties

The game supports both unstructured play and efficiency-focused approaches.
